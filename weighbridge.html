Weighbridge & Load Control

Information about vehicle weighing, legal limits, enforcement, and regional weighbridge locations.

Know your Weights

Why enforcing load and dimension limits is vital for road safety and infrastructure longevity.

Why control overloading?

Overloading accelerates damage to roads, culverts and bridges, increases risk of mechanical failures and traffic accidents, and leads to unfair competition. Enforcing legal limits preserves the network and improves overall safety.

Dangers of overloading

  • Increased braking distance and instability
  • Accelerated pavement and structure deterioration
  • Higher chance of tyre and mechanical failure

What the law says

Legal axle and vehicle load limits are set under the Road Traffic and Transport Act (Act 22 of 1999) and include gross vehicle mass and dimensional limits. Example limits: width 2.6 m, single vehicle length 12.5 m, articulated 18.5 m, height 4.3 m, and permissible Gross Vehicle Mass up to 56 000 kg (regulated by the bridge formula).

Weighing procedures and requirements

  • Driver must present driver’s licence with Professional Authorisation (PA)
  • Consignment note, transport permits and operators card
  • Vehicles exceeding 3.5 tons (with or without cargo) should be weighed

What happens at the weighbridge

Vehicles are weighed on a platform; results are printed and checked. Non-compliance can result in on-the-spot fines, detention until loads are adjusted, or court action for severe overloads.
