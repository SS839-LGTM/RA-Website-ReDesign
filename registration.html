Start registration (NaTIS Online) How to register

Register a new vehicle

All vehicles used on public roads must be registered. Registration must be completed within 21 days from when liability arises (sale / import / transfer). Bring the following where applicable:

  • Completed Application for Registration and Licensing (available at NaTIS offices or via NaTIS Online).
  • Proof of identity (certified ID or valid Namibian driving licence).
  • Manufacturing certificate (new vehicles) or registration certificate from previous owner (used vehicles).
  • Customs & excise documents for imports (SAD 500 / release order).
  • Police clearance for used imports (where required).
  • Roadworthy certificate (if applicable).

If the buyer does not register within 21 days, penalties and arrears may apply.

Transfer of ownership (sale / donation)

  • Completed transfer form and application for registration.
  • Original registration certificate or proof of ownership.
  • Certified ID copies for buyer and seller (or company documents).
  • Police clearance where required (90 days validity stated on some cases).
  • All outstanding fees must be cleared before transfer.

Submit transfer applications in person at your NaTIS office — some offices may accept bookings via phone or NaTIS Online.

Duplicate documents

If a registration certificate or other document is lost or destroyed, the owner (or authorised representative with an affidavit) may apply for a duplicate. Requirements include:

  • Completed application form for duplicates.
  • Affidavit (police or commissioner of oaths) where required.
  • Certified ID copy of applicant or written authorisation from owner.

Example fee: Duplicate registration certificate — N$151.00 (subject to change; confirm at your NaTIS office).

Deregister / Scrap a vehicle

To deregister a vehicle (export/scrap/sold abroad or destroyed), submit the approved deregistration form and supporting documents (affidavit, police report if stolen, registration certificate if available). Penalties may apply for expired licences.

Roadworthy testing & issuing

Book roadworthy testing at designated NaTIS centres. Typical charges noted on some NaTIS pages:
