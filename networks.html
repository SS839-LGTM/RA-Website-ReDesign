Custodian of Namibia's road network — data, maps, and management systems that keep the network safe and efficient.

Road Network

The Roads Authority is custodian of the national road network. Below you will find the systems, data and maps we maintain to plan, maintain and prioritise Namibia's roads and structures.

Road Management System (RMS)

The RMS stores network classifications, historic and current condition data, and supports planning and maintenance decision-making. RMS outputs are used to produce network maps, strategic reports and planning datasets.

Request network data

Bridge Management System (BMS)

The BMS provides inventory and condition assessments for bridges and culverts across Namibia. A visual assessment (2008) covered 499 bridges, 953 major culverts and 1 303 culverts and informed prioritisation and budget planning.

Estimated five-year budget (BMS): Routine maintenance N$8,100,000; Repairs N$12,300,000; Total N$20,400,000.

Maps & GIS

Road Classification

We maintain a formal classification system used for planning and funding. Categories include:

  • Trunk Roads (TR) — primary inter‑urban routes
  • Regional Roads (R) — connect towns and districts
  • District & Access Roads (D) — local access and lower hierarchy roads

Network overview

The RMS contains the authoritative network inventory and condition records used by the Roads Authority to allocate maintenance budgets and prioritise works. If you require specialist exports (shapefiles, kmz, or attribute tables) please contact our data team.
